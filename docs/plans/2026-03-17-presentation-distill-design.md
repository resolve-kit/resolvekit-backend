# Presentation Distill Design

**Goal:** Strip the presentation page down so the core message lands faster: in-app resolution is the next support surface, and ResolveKit has a credible wedge to own it.

## Core Purpose

This page has one job: create belief. It should not feel like a product landing page, a dense internal memo, or a long-form market report. It should feel like a short category brief that can also support a live walkthrough.

## Distill Decisions

### Keep strong

- `What changes`
- `Go-to-market`
- `Unit economics`

These remain the primary chapters because they carry the thesis, the distribution logic, and the business case.

### Compress hard

- `Proof`
- `Market`
- `Competition`
- `Durability`

These become support chapters. They should confirm the story, not compete with it.

## Section Changes

### Unit economics

- Keep the dark business-case block as the main visual.
- Remove repeated economics explanation outside the disclosure.
- Reduce supporting proof to the minimum needed to reinforce the business case.
- Keep the detailed cost build inside one disclosure.

### Proof

- Keep the hackathon win and production-interest signal.
- Reduce synergy content to one compact support block.
- Remove any feeling that this chapter is equal in weight to the core three.

### Market

- Keep a short category thesis above the fold.
- Push most data into progressive disclosure.
- Reduce the amount of visible market copy and card repetition.

### Competition

- Keep the position statement.
- Compress the visible section into one contrast block.
- Keep price indication visible in that block so the section still carries market realism at a glance.
- Move the full public competitor detail into a disclosure or smaller snapshot.
- Preserve enough specificity to show market awareness without creating spreadsheet drag.

### Durability

- Make this a short closing argument.
- One card, one paragraph, at most two bullets.
- It should feel like a final sentence, not another major chapter.

## Content Standard

- Remove repeated explanations.
- Remove hedging phrases and throat-clearing.
- Shorten body copy wherever a shorter sentence preserves meaning.
- If a sentence repeats what a title already says, cut it.
