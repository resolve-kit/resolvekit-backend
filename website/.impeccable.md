## Design Context

### Users
ResolveKit is evaluated by product, support, and engineering buyers at software teams shipping apps with repeatable support issues. They are trying to reduce avoidable ticket volume, resolve known blockers in-product, and keep operational control over what automation can explain, run, or escalate.

### Brand Personality
Calm, credible, operator-minded. The interface should feel trustworthy, product-aware, and commercially serious rather than flashy or consumer-chat oriented. The emotional goal is confidence through clarity.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-mode by default with a restrained technical polish: soft blue infrastructure cues, clean surfaces, and visible structure. Preserve the current positioning and tone. Anti-reference: generic AI support widgets, empty hype, invented social proof, and startup-land gradient theatrics.

### Design Principles
1. Make product reality visible: show what the agent sees, what it can resolve, and what operators can verify.
2. Favor operational proof over hype: use workflows, traces, controls, and in-product outcomes instead of logos, vanity metrics, or invented testimonials.
3. Keep the message buyer-comprehensible: explain the support motion in plain language before talking about SDKs or implementation details.
4. Preserve trust with restraint: calm hierarchy, concrete copy, and clear guardrails should do more work than decorative effects.
